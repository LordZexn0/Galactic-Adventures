Basic Information

The Planetary Orbit package offered by Galactic Adventures is designed to take you on a once-in-a-lifetime journey to the edge of space. The flight will last approximately 28 hours, during which you will have the chance to experience the breathtaking beauty of our planet from a unique perspective.

Our vehicle will reach an altitude of around Geostationary orbit (GEO), 22,236 miles, above the Earth's surface, providing you with stunning views of the Earth and the surrounding cosmos. You will experience weightlessness and have the chance to float freely within the vehicle, taking in the incredible sights around you. As well as orbit the whole Earth.

Training

At Galactic Adventures, we believe that thorough preparation is the key to a successful and safe Planetary Orbit experience. That's why we offer comprehensive training programs that begin one and a half years before your trip.

Our team of expert trainers will work with you to ensure that you are physically and mentally prepared for your journey. This includes monitoring your diet and exercise regimen, as well as providing you with simulations to familiarize yourself with the equipment and procedures used in space travel.

Some of the key elements of our training program include:

  • Reduced-Gravity Aircraft simulation to help you get used to the sensation of weightlessness.
  • Flight Safety & Operations, to ensure you are fully equipped to handle the operational aspects of space travel.
  • Land and Water Survival, to equip you with the skills and knowledge you need in case of an emergency or evacuation.
  • Three 15-minute zero gravity flights, to give you a taste of what it's like to float in space.

At Galactic Adventures, we are dedicated to providing you with the best possible training to make your Planetary Orbit experience unforgettable.

Launch

The launch of the Planetary Orbit package offered by Galactic Adventures is a highly choreographed event that requires the coordination of many different teams and systems. Our experienced launch team works closely with organizations such as JPL and Boeing to ensure a smooth and safe launch.

On launch day, the vehicle will be fueled and undergo final checks before being transported to the launch pad. Our launch team will closely monitor the weather and make any necessary adjustments to the launch timeline to ensure the safety of all crew members.

Once the countdown reaches zero, the engines will ignite, propelling the vehicle into the sky. You will feel the g-forces as the vehicle gains speed, and soon you will be on your way to the edge of space. The launch is a once-in-a-lifetime experience, and you will never forget the thrill of leaving the Earth behind and venturing into the great unknown.

Recovery

The recovery process for Galactic Adventures' Planetary Orbit package is an equally important aspect of your space journey. Our experienced recovery team works closely with JPL and Boeing to ensure a safe and smooth return to Earth.

After 24 hours in orbit, the vehicle will begin its descent back to the Earth. The engines will engage to slow the vehicle and guide it back to a predetermined landing site. Throughout the descent, the recovery team will closely monitor the vehicle and make any necessary adjustments to ensure a safe landing.

Upon landing, the vehicle will be recovered and the astronauts will undergo medical evaluations to ensure they are in good health after their journey. They will also undergo debriefing to discuss their experiences and provide valuable feedback to help improve future missions.

The recovery process marks the end of an incredible adventure, but the memories and experiences gained from your journey will last a lifetime. Galactic Adventures is committed to ensuring a safe and memorable experience for all of our customers, and the recovery process is just one part of that commitment.
